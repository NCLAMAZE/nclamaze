Understanding what “Lamaze” is!

This program is named after Dr Fernand Lamaze, a pioneer in Natural Childbirth, who was a French Obstetrician practicing in Paris. He once heard of a conference to be held in Leningard based on Psychoprophylactic pain relief in labor. He went to Russia to learn this technique and was impressed that by 1953, 3 lakh deliveries had been carried out using this breathing technique, whereby, 80% to 90% were painless deliveries.

This breathing was based on Pavlov’s experiment of building a conditioned reflex. Likewise when women consciously and regularly practiced specific breathing patterns to be used in labor, they built a conditioned reflex that made them spontaneously respond with learnt breathing when labor began. Dr Lamaze returned to France and taught the breathing to the Western World. It became popularly referred to as Lamaze Breathing.

The goal of Lamaze is to increase a mother's confidence in her ability to give birth. These classes help pregnant women understand how to cope with pain in ways that both facilitate labor and promote comfort, including relaxation techniques, positions and massage.
